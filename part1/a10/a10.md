## Chosen Offline Privacy Technique: Differential Privacy

Differential privacy is a mathematical privacy technique used to protect individual data when conducting dataset analysis. It uses mathematical mechanisms like the *'Leplace mechanism'* to add “noise” such as randomisation to the data in order to preserve individual anonymity, in the case when personal information is exposed. [1]  

*A real-world example is the US Census Bureau, which used differential privacy to release the 2020 census results. This ensures that individual respondents cannot be identified while still carrying out relevant data analysis [1]*
